\chapter{Experimental Evaluation}
\label{chap:experiments}

\section{Evaluation Strategy}
All planned experiments use curved headlight surfaces. The target polishing region is specified before demonstration and evaluation; autonomous region or defect detection is not treated as a contribution. The evaluation directly follows the three research questions in Chapter~\ref{chap:introduction}:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item compare force-action interfaces under a fixed force-history representation;
  \item compare motion--force representations under a fixed trajectory action and admittance controller;
  \item compare residual precision policies under a fixed FAIRE imitation and execution stack.
\end{enumerate}
This staged design changes one interface at a time. It avoids attributing a performance difference to the GRU when the compared method also uses a different action, or attributing a residual-policy effect to a changed admittance controller.

\section{Experimental Setup}
The robot carries a polishing tool and a six-axis force/torque sensor, and an RGB camera supplies visual context. The handheld teaching device described in Chapter~\ref{chap:demonstration_collection} records synchronized pose, wrench, and image demonstrations. Each robot trial begins from a standardized pre-contact pose and ends at the policy horizon, task-completion condition, or safety limit. Surface appearance is recorded under controlled conditions before and after polishing.

The source FAIRE manuscript does not identify the robot, tool, force/torque sensor, camera, abrasive, workpiece set, sampling rates, control rates, number of demonstrations, or number of evaluation trials. It also does not state the target-region protocol, termination criteria, or safety thresholds. These fields are \todoexp{TBD and must be filled from the final implementation and experiment logs}.

\section{Experimental Notation}
The six-dimensional motion state, three-dimensional force, and six-axis wrench are
\begin{align}
  q_t&=[x_t,y_t,z_t,\omega_{x,t},\omega_{y,t},\omega_{z,t}],\\
  f_t&=[F_{x,t},F_{y,t},F_{z,t}],\\
  w_t&=[F_{x,t},F_{y,t},F_{z,t},\tau_{x,t},\tau_{y,t},\tau_{z,t}].
\end{align}
The proposed action is a horizon of 9D motion--force references,
\begin{equation}
  a_t=[q^{\mathrm{ref}}_{t:t+H},f^{\mathrm{des}}_{t:t+H}],
  \label{eq:experimental_action}
\end{equation}
and the force-history observation is \(w_{t-K:t}\). Unless an ablation states otherwise, a force-aware variant uses a split motion--force representation and the GRU-based six-axis wrench-history encoder.

\section{Metrics}
\subsection{Force-Trajectory Tracking}
Normal-force root-mean-square error is evaluated after aligning an executed trial with its demonstration-derived reference by normalized task progress:
\begin{equation}
  F_n\text{-RMSE}
  =
  \sqrt{\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N}
  \left(F_{n,i}^{\mathrm{meas}}-F_{n,i}^{\mathrm{des}}\right)^2}.
  \label{eq:force_rmse}
\end{equation}
Force correlation (F-Corr) measures temporal similarity between the aligned measured and desired normal-force profiles. Overshoot is the maximum positive value of \(F_n^{\mathrm{meas}}-F_n^{\mathrm{des}}\). Contact Loss is the fraction of evaluated samples below a predefined normal-force contact threshold.

\subsection{Alignment and Process Quality}
Normal-alignment error (NAE) is the angular difference in Eq.~\eqref{eq:normal_alignment_error}. Polishing-quality improvement (PQI) compares controlled pre- and post-polishing measurements. The FAIRE manuscript leaves PQI measurement-agnostic so that it can be instantiated with verified gloss, haze, roughness, or image-based surface-quality measurements. Consistency measures the spatial uniformity of the post-polishing finish, and Task Time is elapsed execution time.

The final evaluation must specify the contact threshold, progress-alignment algorithm, surface-normal estimator, PQI instrument or image protocol, spatial sampling pattern, consistency definition, units, aggregation statistic, confidence interval, and treatment of failed trials. These items are \todoexp{TBD}.

\section{RQ1: Force-Action Interface and Admittance Execution}
The first ablation asks how the demonstrated force trajectory should be represented by the policy and used during execution. Pose-only Policy excludes force observations and force output. Force-Observation Pose Policy adds wrench history but still predicts pose only. Fixed-Force Admittance uses a constant target \(F_0\). Phase-Force Admittance uses a demonstration-derived phase profile \(\bar f^{\mathrm{demo}}(s)\). One-Step Force Action predicts only the current desired force, whereas FAIRE predicts a force trajectory aligned with the motion horizon.

\section{RQ2: Motion--Force Representation}
The second ablation asks how force history should be encoded when every method predicts the same 9D trajectory action and uses the same admittance controller. Unified Current MLP concatenates the current motion and force sample. Split Current MLP uses separate current-sample branches. Split History MLP flattens or otherwise encodes a window without recurrence. LSTM and GRU variants use recurrent force-history encoders. The motion encoder, fusion network, policy backbone, training split, action horizon, and controller must remain fixed.

\section{Protocol and Statistical Reporting}
Trial ordering should be randomized or counterbalanced across methods to reduce confounding from abrasive wear, surface condition, and operator setup. Methods should use identical target regions where repeated processing is physically valid; otherwise, comparable regions and a documented assignment scheme are required. Demonstrations, hyperparameter selection, and final test trials must be separated.

For each metric, the thesis should report the number of independent workpieces or regions, trials per condition, central tendency, dispersion, confidence intervals, and the planned statistical test. Paired analysis is preferable when methods can be evaluated on matched regions. Failed or safety-stopped trials must remain visible through success and intervention counts rather than being silently removed from continuous metrics.
